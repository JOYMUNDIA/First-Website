Letter of Intent

Türkiye Scholarships is a highly competitive scholarship programme which annualy receives more than 150.000 applications from all over the world. For this reason, it is very important that you explain yourself and why you should be chosen for a scholarship in order to be able to stand out among candidates with similar characteristics. The letter of intent and the academic study proposal mentioned below, are the fields that are offered to you so that you express yourself better than the documents you submitted in the application. Your performance in these areas will be effective in evaluating your application. Therefore, we would like to point out that filling in these fields carefully will make a difference from other candidates and will affect your evaluation process positively. You can fill in these fields in the language you express yourself.
Filling the letter of intent field is mandatory for all candidates. Undergraduate and graduate candidates can fill in this field in a language that they can express themselves comfortably. PhD candidates must fill this field in Turkish or English.

Letter of Intent

In this section, it is expected to explain following details: your academic and social experiences relating the field you want to take an education, your reasons for choosing Turkey for study and the importance of your future plans for your education and scholarship in Turkey.

In this section you are asked to give examples of general knowledge about an academic study in Turkey. The proposal that you propose in this section is not binding. You can work on another subject you choose later in Turkey. However, the proposal you put forward in this section will be used to assess your ability to put forward your academic competence and problems. You can write a case study in the language you express yourself. (The PhD Candidates have to fill out this field in Turkish or English.)

1- Subject of study

2- Research Question (Up to 15 words)

3- Analysis of the Problem (at least 75, maximum 300 words)(In this section, briefly describe the topic and your theory (concept map) to be used in the study.)

4- Research Method (minimum 50 maximum 300 words)"(Give information about the academic research methods you intend to use in this study. For example, in-depth interviews, observations, surveys, experiments, etc.) For example, in-depth interviews, observations, surveys, experiments, etc.)

5- Preliminary Answer to the Research Question (at least 75, maximum 300 words)

6- Academic Contribution of the Study (minimum 75, maximum 300 words) (Explain how this study will contribute to your academic field.)

7- Literature Review (at least 75, maximum 300 words)(Please write down the main academic sources written in this study area.)

Additional Item

If you have a subject in your application documents that you think is not clear enough and you want to explain, or if you want to draw the attention of the evaluation committee, please briefly explain in this section.

Application Conditions

Application may take 30 to 50 minutes based on whether you have prepared your documents and read the Application Manual of the scholarship programme you are applying for.

Before initiating your application, you are required to read carefully and accept the following text. When you tick the box at the end of the text, you will be deemed to have accepted all the rules.

!!! Turkish citizens and those who have lost Turkish citizenship for any reason cannot apply for Türkiye Scholarships.

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR APPLICATION

Please make sure that you have prepared all necessary information and documents before initiating your application. It would be very useful if the documents are available on your computer in PDF, JPEG, PNG, and GIF formats such that each document does not exceed 5 MB.

1. 1. Diploma or Student Certificate
A diploma to prove your previous academic degree with respect to the scholarship degree you are applying for, or a student certificate which indicates that you are still continuing with your studies,

2. Exam/Test Results
Presenting the results of national and international academic and language exams will improve your chances to win scholarship.

3. Academic Record (Transcript)
Academic Record (Transcript) with your grades from your previous academic degree with respect to the scholarship degree you are applying for (for high school, last three years);

4. Identification Document
An official ID document issued by the public authorities in the country of your identification, such as ID card, passport, driver’s license, or birth certificate;

5. Photo
A head shot that clearly shows your facial features, taken within the last 6 months;

INSTRUCTIONS FOR APPLICATION

  • Application may take 30 to 50 minutes based on whether you have prepared your documents and read the Application Manual of the scholarship programme you are applying for.
  • You can see the announcements and check your private messages through the Application System. All available scholarship programmes, application dates and brief programme descriptions can be found on the home page.
  • Some programmes may be displayed as inactive or not listed at all based on your nationality, educational background and age. Please visit our web site for detailed information.
  • Information you provide will be saved as you complete each step. Hence, you may take a break, and complete the remaining steps later.
  • When you reach the final page of the application form, having entered all information and uploaded all required documents, you will see the summary of the application form. Please, check the application summary carefully. You may finalise your application if your details and documents are complete and accurate.
  • You can check the status of your application, assessment results, and placement procedures on this page.
